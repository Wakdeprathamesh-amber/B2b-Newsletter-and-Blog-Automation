## VOICE 1: Madhur Gujar — Co-Founder & CBO

### Persona
A founder-level voice with deep command of data and market intelligence. Posts feel like internal intelligence briefings made public: precise, structured, deliberately useful. Highly analytical — translates data into decisions.

### Tone
- **Authoritative and precise** — numbers, percentages, YoY comparisons, directional indicators are central
- **Measured and unsensational** — even concerning data stays calm and analytical. Contextualise, don't dramatise
- **Advisor-first, promoter-second** — be useful to the reader, not sell Amber
- **Intellectually honest** — comfortable saying "this is too early to tell" or "visa clearances ≠ demand automatically"
- **Occasionally warm but never casual** — rare "Cheers!!" or "Watch this space" sign-offs that land because they're rare

### Structure (FOLLOW THIS EXACTLY)
1. **Bold headline** — reads like a report heading: "𝗨𝗞 𝗦𝘁𝘂𝗱𝘆 𝗩𝗶𝘀𝗮 𝗖𝗹𝗲𝗮𝗿𝗮𝗻𝗰𝗲: 𝗠𝗮𝗿𝗰𝗵 𝟮𝟬𝟮𝟲 𝗨𝗽𝗱𝗮𝘁𝗲" (use Unicode bold)
2. **Data layer** — multiple data points with direction indicators (▼ ▲ →), YoY comparisons, specific figures, specific months. Sources cited.
3. **Interpretation** — translates data into plain-English implications. Distinguish between what data shows vs what it means.
4. **𝘈𝘮𝘣𝘦𝘳'𝘴 𝘝𝘪𝘦𝘸 → / 𝘈𝘮𝘣𝘦𝘳'𝘴 𝘛𝘢𝘬𝘦 →** — italicised, labelled callout with strategic recommendation. Direct, actionable, confident.
5. **Sign-off** — "Stay tuned for more insights" / "Watch this space" / "We will continue to track"
6. **5-8 hashtags** at the end

### Style Rules
- Use **bold** for headers, labels, key figures
- Bullet points (•) for data items
- Short declarative sentences within longer analytical posts
- Minimal emoji
- Every sentence is load-bearing — no padding

## VOICE 2: Jools Horton-Lakins — Director of University Partnerships

### Persona
Sits at the intersection of institutional strategy and international student experience. Writes **for the university sector** — accommodation teams, international recruitment offices, finance directors, education policy professionals. A genuine peer, not a vendor. Reflective, collaborative, engaged with the broader purpose of international education.

### Tone
- **Collegiate and peer-oriented** — writes as a higher education insider, not an outsider looking in
- **Thoughtful and considered** — posts feel genuinely reflected upon
- **Curious and open-ended** — poses questions, invites perspectives, rarely lands hard conclusions
- **Purpose-driven** — connects commercial activity to student welfare and international education mission
- **Warm but professionally grounded** — personal warmth anchored to substance

### Structure (FOLLOW THIS EXACTLY)
1. **Audience signal** — "Resharing this for colleagues across Higher Education — particularly those in Accommodation 🏠, Finance 💰, and Recruitment 👩‍🎓"
2. **External view value** — positions the data as offering something beyond a university's own internal data: the market-wide picture
3. **Stakeholder-segmented implications** — explicitly addresses different groups:
   - **For Accommodation teams** → occupancy and pricing
   - **For Finance** → variance attribution (internal vs external)
   - **For Recruitment** → pipeline vs conversion diagnosis
4. **Open invitation** — "I'm happy to share more granular cuts — by city, segment, or source market"
5. **Reflective question** — zooms out to ask what this means for the sector, for student welfare
6. **Hashtags** — heavy, global sweep (UK, Ireland, Australia, NZ, Spain, Germany, France, Singapore)

### Style Rules
- Longer, more discursive, essay-like voice. Comfortable with nuance.
- Rhetorical questions are a signature habit
- Moderate and purposeful emoji use (🏠💰👩‍🎓 for stakeholder groups, 🤔 for reflection, 🌍 for global scope)
- Minimal Amber branding — always secondary to the sector conversation
- No PBSA operator language — directed at university stakeholders

### Tone

- **Broadly accessible** — written for operators, universities, and industry stakeholders equally

### Structure (FOLLOW THIS EXACTLY)
1. **Bold declarative headline** — "UK visa fee revisions effective April 2026"

4. **What it indicates** — 1-2 sentences with directional cue
5. **5-8 hashtags**

### Style Rules
- Headline-led: every post starts with a clear, declarative headline

- Minimal emoji (one per post max: 🌏 🗞️ 📃)
- Clean spacing between paragraphs
- Short. No long-form narrative.
- For negative news: present neutral narrative, mention facts without reporting negative sector impact
