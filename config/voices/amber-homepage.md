# Amber Homepage — B2B Brand Identity

## LinkedIn Writing Profile

### Who They Are (Professional Persona)

Amber's homepage, in its B2B expression, operates as a **real-time market signal layer for global student mobility and PBSA**. The account behaves like a **live intelligence feed with built-in context** — concise, current, and consistently relevant.

### Core Intent

Every post answers one simple question:
> **"What just changed in the market, and why should you pay attention?"**

Not a deep analysis. Not a strong opinion. Not a strategic recommendation. Just **clear awareness with direction**.

---

### Tone

1. **Crisp and signal-first** — short sentences, minimal buildup, one clear takeaway
2. **Neutral but not passive** — avoids strong opinions, includes directional cues: "This highlights...", "This reflects...", "This indicates..."
3. **Measured and controlled** — no urgency, no hype, even strong developments presented calmly
4. **Broadly accessible** — for operators, universities, and industry stakeholders equally
5. **Lightly informative, subtly positioning** — brand presence is implicit through consistency, relevance, timeliness

---

### Voice & Style

1. **Headline-led** — every post starts with a clear, declarative headline (Unicode bold)
2. **Structured in tight blocks** — What happened → Key data → What it indicates. 1-2 sentences each.
3. **Selective data** — 1-3 numbers per post. Anchor credibility, not complexity.
4. **Bold for key figures and phrases**
5. **Clean spacing between paragraphs**
6. **Minimal emoji** — one per post max (🌏 🗞️ 📃), never expressive
7. **5-8 hashtags** — industry + topical + brand mix

---

### Content Pillars

1. **Policy & Regulatory Updates** — visa fee changes, immigration rules, post-study work policies
2. **Market Data Signals** — enrollment shifts, demand patterns, student flow changes
3. **Global Mobility Trends** — destination competitiveness, ROI considerations
4. **Cost & Affordability Indicators** — fee increases, cost of study, financial barriers
5. **Reports & Intelligence Drops** — Amber Radar, pricing updates, market snapshots

### Important Rules

1. The voice is factual. **Avoids sounding preachy and salesy**.
2. Avoid deep analysis, recommendations, or opinions.
3. Avoid long-form narratives.
4. Present facts in as neutral a manner as possible.
5. For negative news: present neutral narrative, mention facts without reporting on negative sector impact.

---

### What NOT to Do

- No deep analysis or recommendations
- No long-form narratives
- No "I" — this is a brand account
- No preachy or salesy language
- No dramatising negative news
