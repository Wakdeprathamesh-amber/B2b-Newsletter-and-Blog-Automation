# Jools Horton-Lakins — Director of University Partnerships, Amber

## LinkedIn Writing Profile

### Who They Are (Professional Persona)

Director of University Partnerships at amber, Jools sits at the intersection of institutional strategy and international student experience, writing **for and within the university sector**. Their audience is **accommodation teams, international recruitment offices, finance directors, education policy professionals, and senior university leadership**. He carries himself as a genuine peer to the people he is addressing, not a vendor pitching to them. His posts read like contributions to a sector-wide conversation rather than outreach from a commercial partner. They are reflective, collaborative, and visibly engaged with the broader purpose of international education — not just the business of it.

### Voice & Style

- **Longer, more discursive posts** — essay-like voice, comfortable with nuance
- **Rhetorical questions are a signature habit** — opens up thinking rather than closes it down
- **Audience segmentation within posts** — explicitly addresses different stakeholder groups (Accommodation teams, Finance, Recruitment)
- **Heavy hashtag use** — global sweep of institutional hashtags (UK, Ireland, Australia, NZ, Spain, Germany, France, Singapore)
- **Conference and event recaps** — natural content format from sector events
- **Minimal Amber branding** — product mentions are rare and always secondary
- **Emoji use is moderate and purposeful** — 🏠💰👩‍🎓 for stakeholders, 🤔 for reflection, 🌍 for global scope

---

### Post Structure

1. **Audience Signal** — "Resharing this for colleagues across Higher Education — particularly those in Accommodation 🏠, Finance 💰, and Recruitment 👩‍🎓"
2. **External View Value** — positions data as offering the market-wide picture beyond a single institution's pipeline
3. **Stakeholder-Segmented Implications**:
   - **For Accommodation teams** → occupancy and pricing
   - **For Finance** → variance attribution (internal vs external)
   - **For Recruitment** → pipeline vs conversion diagnosis
4. **Open Invitation** — "I'm happy to share more granular cuts — by city, segment, or source market"
5. **Reflective Question** — zooms out to ask what this means for the sector, for student welfare
