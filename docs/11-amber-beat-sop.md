# Amber Beat Newsroom Blog — Standard Operating Procedure

> Source of truth for how the Amber Beat weekly newsroom blog works. This document was provided by the content team and should be referenced by all automation and generation tools.

---

## What It Is

The **Amber Beat Newsroom Blog** is a weekly roundup covering the most important news across higher education, international and domestic students, and the student housing/PBSA sector in the UK, US, Australia, Canada, and Europe.

Published **four times a month (once per week)**.

Each news item is:
- Carefully verified and reviewed to fully understand the facts and data
- Summarised into **21–25 words**, focusing only on the most critical insights (key figures, data points, or facts)
- Concluded with a hyperlink to the original article, formatted as **(Source)**

## Topics Covered in Priority Order

### Priority 1 — Policy & Regulatory Shifts (Student Mobility & Immigration)

- Higher education–sponsored study visa data
- Student visa policies & eligibility criteria
- Post-study work rights and transition pathways (e.g., student → work visa)
- Higher Education Student enrolment caps / intake restrictions
- Country- or region-specific restrictions on student mobility
- Changes to work visa regimes impacting students (e.g., H-1B, F-1 visa, PSW, OPT)
- Immigration processing rules, documentation changes, and compliance updates

### Priority 2 — International Student Enrolment (Demand Trends)

- Higher education application volumes (e.g., UCAS and direct applications)
- Student visa issuance and approval data
- Total enrolments and year-on-year enrolment trends
- New enrolments by academic year (intake-wise analysis)
- Source markets / country-wise student distribution
- Conversion funnel: applications → offers → visas → enrolments

### Priority 3 — University-Related

- Global and regional university rankings (e.g., QS, THE)
- University-specific enrolment data and intake trends
- Performance of key university groups (e.g., Russell Group in the UK)
- Institutional funding updates (public funding, budget changes, deficits/surpluses)
- University-level policy changes impacting admissions or international students
- Strategic developments (campus expansions, partnerships, program launches/closures)
- International student mix and dependency at institution level

### Priority 4 — Student Housing (PBSA) Supply & Investment

- Acquisitions, mergers, and portfolio transactions
- Asset sales and capital recycling activity
- New developments (announcements, pipeline additions)
- Construction progress and project timelines
- Completions and delivery of new beds (supply additions)
- Bed capacity growth (city-level and portfolio-level)
- Conversions (e.g., hotels/residential → PBSA)

## Manual Workflow (what the automation replaces)

1. Create Google Doc "amber Beat `<month>` Wk `<number>`"
2. Google search per keyword + Tools → Past Week filter
3. Read through news for relevance and credibility
4. ChatGPT prompt: "Summarize into 21–25 word news headline, neutral, with facts/figures"
5. Edit for accuracy and length
6. Order by priority
7. Content team review meeting reorders
